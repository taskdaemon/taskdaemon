# Coffee Brewing Guide

## Introduction

Coffee brewing is both an **art** and a *science*. Whether you're a ***beginner*** or an experienced barista, understanding different brewing methods can help you make the perfect cup.

## Popular Brewing Methods

### Ordered List: Steps for Pour-Over Coffee

1. Heat water to **195-205°F** (90-96°C)
2. Grind coffee beans to a *medium* consistency
3. Place filter in dripper and rinse with hot water
4. Add **15g** of coffee per **250ml** of water
5. Pour water in a ***slow, circular motion***
6. Total brew time should be **2:30-3:00 minutes**

### Unordered List: Equipment You'll Need

- **Gooseneck kettle** for precise pouring
- *Digital scale* for accuracy
- Coffee grinder (preferably ***burr grinder***)
- Filter papers
- Your favorite coffee beans
- Timer or stopwatch

## Coffee-to-Water Ratios

Different brewing methods require different ratios:

- **Espresso**: 1:2 ratio (*very concentrated*)
- **Pour-over**: 1:15 to 1:17 ratio
- **French Press**: 1:12 to 1:15 ratio
- ***Cold Brew***: 1:5 to 1:8 ratio (concentrated)

## Pro Tips

1. Always use **freshly roasted** beans
   - Check the *roast date*
   - Use within ***2-4 weeks*** of roasting
2. Water quality matters
   - Use *filtered water*
   - Avoid **distilled** water
3. Consistency is key
   - Keep your grind size ***uniform***
   - Maintain steady water temperature
